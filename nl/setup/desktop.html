Stream TVMaster on Windows & Mac

Whether you’re multitasking at a desk or piping HDMI to a projector, this guide shows how to watch TVMaster via VLC media player and Kodi on desktop. You’ll learn playlist import, EPG configuration, DVR recording, and picture optimisation for every league.

Platforms: Windows 10/11, macOS 12+, Linux (VLC/Kodi) Setup time: 6 minutes · Skill: Beginner Requirements: VLC or Kodi installed, TVMaster playlist & EPG URLs

Why desktop streaming matters

  • Analysts can watch multiple fixtures simultaneously with VLC’s multi-window support.
  • Creatives record broadcast-grade clips for highlights (respect rights) using VLC and OBS.
  • Kodi enables remote-friendly navigation for HTPC setups with Xbox or PS controllers.
  • HDMI connects PCs to big screens when Smart TVs don’t support sideloading.

Watch via VLC (fastest method)

  1. Installeer VLC – Download from videolan.org and install on Windows or Mac.
  2. Open network stream – Launch VLC, go to Media ▸ Open Network Stream.
  3. Paste the playlist – Enter the TVMaster M3U URL and click Play. VLC loads the first channel instantly.
  4. Browse channels – Press Ctrl+L (Cmd+L on Mac) to open the channel playlist. Double-click to switch between Sky Sports, beIN, NBC, etc.
  5. Record highlights – Enable View ▸ Advanced Controls, then press the red record button to capture clips locally.

Alternate: Kodi + PVR IPTV Simple Client

Enable add-on: In Kodi, go to Add-ons ▸ My add-ons ▸ PVR Clients, enable PVR IPTV Simple Client.

Configureer: Set Location ▸ Remote path, paste the TVMaster M3U URL and XMLTV EPG URL, then restart Kodi.

Big-screen mode: Kodi’s TV section pulls channel logos, EPG grids, and catch-up tags—perfect for living-room HTPC lovers.

Optimisation & troubleshooting

Audio delay? In VLC, use Tools ▸ Track Synchronisation to nudge audio ±0.1s until commentary lines up.

Choppy playback? Switch to Tools ▸ Preferences ▸ Input/Codecs ▸ Hardware-accelerated decoding ▸ Automatic, and raise network caching to 1500 ms.

Playlist not updating? Clear VLC cache (delete ml.xspf), or in Kodi hit TV ▸ Guide ▸ Clear data after renewing your subscription.
